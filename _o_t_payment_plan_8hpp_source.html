00145 /*
00146  OTPaymentPlan
00147 
00148  This instrument is signed by two parties or more (the first one, I think...)
00149 
00150  While processing payment, BOTH parties to a payment plan will be loaded up and
00151  their signatures will be checked against the original plan, which is saved as a
00152  cron receipt.
00153 
00154  There is also a "current version" of the payment plan, which contains updated info
00155  from processing, and is signed by the server.
00156 
00157  BOTH the original version, and the updated version, are sent to EACH user whenever
00158  a payment is processed, as his receipt. This way you have the user's signature on
00159  the terms, and the server's signature whenever it carries out the terms. A receipt
00160  with both is placed in the inbox of both users after any action.
00161 
00162  As with cheques, the server can use the receipts in the inboxes, plus the last agreed
00163  balance, to prove the current balance of any account. The user removes the receipt from
00164  his inbox by accepting it and, in the process, performing a new balance agreement.
00165 
00166  THIS MEANS that the OT server can carry out the terms of contracts!  So far, at least,
00167  cheques, trades, payment plans... as long as everything is signed off, we're free and
00168  clear under the same triple-signed system that account transfer uses. (The Users cannot
00169  repudiate their own signatures later, and the server can prove all balances with the
00170  user's own signature.)
00171 
00172  Of course, either side is free to CANCEL a payment plan, or to leave their account bereft
00173  of funds and prone to failed payments. But if they cancel, their signature will appear
00174  on the cancellation request, and the recipient gets a copy of it in his inbox. And if
00175  the funds are insufficient, the plan will keep trying to charge, leaving failure notices
00176  in both inboxes when such things occur.
00177 
00178  You could even have the server manage an issuer account, backed in payment plan revenue,
00179  that would form a new asset type that can then be traded on markets. (The same as you can
00180  have the server manage the issuer account for a basket currency now, which is backed with
00181  reserve accounts managed by the server, and you can then trade the basket currency on markets.)
00182  */
